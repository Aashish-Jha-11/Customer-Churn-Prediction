# Churn Drivers

## Support Call Volume
Customers who contact support frequently often churn within the next two quarters. A high call count usually signals unresolved technical issues or billing confusion. Proactive outreach after the third call in a 30-day window reduces churn risk meaningfully.

Source: Gartner CX Trends Report 2023.

## Payment Delay
Average payment delay beyond 14 days correlates with disengagement. Delay is often an early warning sign before a full cancellation, since customers who stop prioritizing timely payment are likely evaluating alternatives.

Source: Bain & Company, The Loyalty Effect.

## Low Total Spend
Low lifetime spend relative to a customer's cohort usually reflects low feature adoption. Customers who do not use what they pay for rarely renew. Lifting engagement is more effective than lowering price.

Source: Harvard Business Review, Reichheld 2014.

## Contract Duration
Month-to-month customers churn at roughly three to five times the rate of annual customers. Longer commitments create psychological and financial switching costs that reduce impulsive cancellation.

Source: Telecom Retention Handbook, 2024.
